# Attorney-Client Privilege & AI Usage Framework

## Core Principle
AI tools used in legal practice are treated as third-party technology providers covered by the agent/instrumentality exception under ABA guidance and state bar ethics opinions.

## Obligations
1. **Reasonable efforts to protect client data**: Use platforms with appropriate data handling practices
2. **Disable model training**: Ensure client inputs are not used to train AI models
3. **Data processing agreements**: Use providers that offer business DPAs and zero-data-retention options
4. **Documentation**: Maintain records of AI usage, data handling practices, and due diligence performed
5. **Client consent**: Include AI usage provisions in engagement letters

## Engagement Letter AI Provision (Summary)
- Frame AI as an efficiency and quality enhancer
- Emphasize attorney supervision of all AI outputs
- Tie data handling to existing confidentiality obligations
- Secure informed client consent
- Note that no client data is used for model training

## Technology Competence
Most jurisdictions now require technology competence under the Rules of Professional Conduct (Comment 8 to Model Rule 1.1). Lawyers must keep abreast of changes in technology relevant to practice.

## Key Guidance
- ABA Formal Opinion 498 (2021): Virtual practice and technology
- ABA Resolution 112 (2019): AI and the practice of law
- State bar opinions vary — always check jurisdiction-specific guidance
